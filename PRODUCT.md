# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Primary users are freelancers, creatives, and job-seekers who need to publish a portfolio quickly and present their work with confidence.  
They usually work from a phone or laptop, often under time pressure before applications, outreach, or client conversations.  
Their main job is to turn scattered project material into one clear portfolio link they can share anywhere.

## Product Purpose
PortfolioForge helps people create and publish a portfolio page that is easy to understand, visually credible, and fast to update.  
The product exists to remove friction between doing the work and showing the work.  
Success means users can publish quickly, maintain their page without technical overhead, and get more quality responses from applications or clients.

## Brand Personality
Calm, credible, human.  
Voice should feel like a practical founder speaking directly, not a generic SaaS ad.  
The emotional goal is confidence and trust, not hype.

## Anti-references
- Overly glossy AI-template aesthetics with repetitive card grids and decorative effects.
- Buzzword-heavy marketing copy that promises vague transformation.
- Interfaces that look technical for the creator but confusing to visitors.
- Heavy glassmorphism, aggressive gradients, and ornamental motion that reduce clarity.

## Design Principles
- Show work clearly before decorating, strong hierarchy and fast comprehension come first.
- Keep the interface grounded, practical copy and purposeful interactions over visual noise.
- Build trust through consistency, predictable navigation, stable spacing rhythm, and readable typography.
- Preserve user ownership, portfolios should feel personal and adaptable, not locked into one visual formula.
- Mobile credibility matters, every key section should read cleanly and feel intentional on small screens.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible interactions across key flows.  
Support reduced-motion preferences for non-essential animation.  
Maintain readable type scales and spacing on mobile, and avoid relying on color alone to convey meaning.
